## Introduction to NFT Royalties
NFT royalties are a game-changer for digital artists, allowing them to earn a percentage of the sale price each time their NFT is resold. This **royalty payment** is typically set by the creator and can range from 5-20% of the sale price. For example, if an artist sets a 10% royalty on an NFT that sells for 1 ETH, they will earn 0.1 ETH each time the NFT is resold.

The impact of NFT royalties on creator economics cannot be overstated. By providing a **passive income stream**, creators can focus on producing high-quality work without worrying about the financial implications. This, in turn, has led to an explosion of innovative and experimental art, as creators are no longer bound by traditional monetization models.

## The Benefits of NFT Royalties
One of the primary benefits of NFT royalties is the ability for creators to earn **recurrent revenue**. This means that they can continue to earn money from their work long after the initial sale, providing a **sustainable income stream**. For instance, a digital artist who sells an NFT for 1 ETH with a 10% royalty can earn an additional 0.1 ETH each time the NFT is resold, potentially generating thousands of dollars in **passive income**.

### How to Set Up NFT Royalties
Setting up NFT royalties is a relatively straightforward process. Creators can use **smart contracts** to specify the royalty percentage and payment terms. For example, they can use platforms like **OpenSea** or **Rarible** to create and manage their NFTs, including setting royalty payments. By following these steps, creators can ensure that they are earning the **royalty payments** they deserve.
