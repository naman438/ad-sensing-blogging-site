## Introduction to Bitcoin Halving
The **Bitcoin halving** is a pre-programmed event that occurs every 210,000 blocks, or approximately every four years. During this event, the **block reward** is cut in half, effectively reducing the amount of new **Bitcoin** entering the market. This reduction in supply is believed to have a direct impact on the **crypto market cycles**, with many investors anticipating a significant price increase following the halving.

The **halving effect** is largely driven by the principles of **supply and demand**. As the supply of new **Bitcoin** decreases, the demand for existing **Bitcoin** increases, leading to a potential price surge. Historically, the **Bitcoin halving** has been followed by a significant price increase, with the 2016 halving event paving the way for the 2017 **crypto market boom**.
